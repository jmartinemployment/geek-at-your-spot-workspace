## What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work more efficiently and to provide reporting information.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, Geek @ Your Spot) are called "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies enable features or functionality to be provided on or through the website (e.g., analytics, interactive content, and advertising).

## How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

### 1. Essential/Necessary Cookies (Always Active)

These cookies are necessary for our website to function properly and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms.

**Examples:**
- Session cookies that remember your login status
- Security cookies that prevent fraudulent activity
- Load balancing cookies that distribute traffic efficiently

**Duration:** Session (deleted when you close your browser) or up to 24 hours

**Can you refuse these cookies?** You can set your browser to block these cookies, but some parts of the website may not function properly.

---

### 2. Analytics/Performance Cookies (Optional)

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our website performance and user experience.

**Services we use:**
- **Google Analytics 4**: Tracks website usage, page views, session duration, and user behavior
- **Hotjar** (if applicable): Heat maps and session recordings to understand user interactions

**Examples of data collected:**
- Pages you visit
- Time spent on each page
- How you arrived at our website
- Links you click
- Device type and browser used

**Duration:** Typically 2 years for Google Analytics cookies

**Can you refuse these cookies?** Yes. These cookies do not identify you personally and are used only for statistical analysis.

---

### 3. Functional/Preference Cookies (Optional)

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization based on your interactions. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

**Examples:**
- Language preference
- Region/location preference
- Font size preferences
- Color scheme/theme preferences

**Duration:** Typically 1 year

**Can you refuse these cookies?** Yes, but refusing these cookies may result in less personalized recommendations and require you to re-enter preferences each visit.

---

### 4. Marketing/Advertising Cookies (Optional)

These cookies track your activity on our website and other websites to deliver personalized advertising. They are set by our advertising partners and help us measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns.

**Services we use:**
- **Google Ads**: Tracks ad performance and enables remarketing
- **Facebook Pixel**: Measures Facebook ad effectiveness and enables remarketing
- **LinkedIn Insight Tag**: Tracks LinkedIn ad performance

**What they do:**
- Show you relevant ads on other websites you visit
- Measure how many people click on our ads
- Prevent you from seeing the same ad too many times
- Help us understand which ads are most effective

**Duration:** Typically 90 days to 2 years

**Can you refuse these cookies?** Yes. Refusing these cookies will not reduce the number of ads you see, but they may be less relevant to your interests.

## How to Manage Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

### Option 1: Through Our Website

[If you have cookie preference center:]
Click the "Cookie Settings" link at the bottom of any page to customize your cookie preferences. You can accept all cookies, reject optional cookies, or customize your preferences by category.

[If you don't have cookie preference center:]
We respect your right to control cookies. While our website does not currently offer a cookie preference center, you can manage cookies through your browser settings (see Option 2 below).

### Option 2: Through Your Browser

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, if you limit the ability of websites to set cookies, you may worsen your overall user experience.

**Popular browser cookie management guides:**

- **Google Chrome**: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  - Guide: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647

- **Mozilla Firefox**: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  - Guide: https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/enhanced-tracking-protection-firefox-desktop

- **Safari (Mac)**: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  - Guide: https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471/mac

- **Microsoft Edge**: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies
  - Guide: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-edge/delete-cookies-in-microsoft-edge-63947406-40ac-c3b8-57b9-2a946a29ae09

- **Safari (iOS)**: Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies
  - Guide: https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT201265

## Appendix: Technical Cookie Details

### Session vs. Persistent Cookies

**Session Cookies:**
- Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser
- Used for essential website functionality
- Example: Shopping cart, login session

**Persistent Cookies:**
- Remain on your device for a set period (days, months, years)
- Used for remembering preferences and tracking
- Example: Language preference, analytics cookies

### First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

**First-Party Cookies:**
- Set by the website you're visiting (geekatyourspot.com)
- Used for website functionality and basic analytics
- Generally considered less privacy-invasive

**Third-Party Cookies:**
- Set by external services (Google, Facebook, etc.)
- Used for advertising and advanced analytics
- Can track you across multiple websites
- More privacy concerns, increasingly restricted by browsers

## Cookie Management for Mobile Devices

### iOS (iPhone/iPad)

**Safari:**
1. Go to Settings → Safari
2. Under Privacy & Security:
   - **Block All Cookies**: Prevents all cookies
   - **Prevent Cross-Site Tracking**: Blocks third-party cookies

**Note:** Blocking all cookies may prevent some websites from working properly.

### Android

**Chrome:**
1. Open Chrome app
2. Tap More (three dots) → Settings → Site Settings → Cookies
3. Choose:
   - Allow cookies
   - Block third-party cookies
   - Block all cookies

**Firefox:**
1. Tap Menu → Settings → Privacy
2. Select tracking protection level

## Glossary of Cookie Terms

**Cookie:** Small text file stored on your device by websites you visit

**Session:** Period from when you open a website to when you close your browser

**Tracking:** Following your online activity across websites and over time

**Remarketing:** Showing you ads based on websites you've previously visited

**Analytics:** Collecting data about how visitors use a website

**Opt-Out:** Choosing not to allow certain cookies or tracking

**Browser:** Software you use to access websites (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)

**Cache:** Temporary storage of website data to speed up loading

**Pixel/Tag:** Tiny invisible image used for tracking (similar to cookies)
